Team and Driver Background

Team History

Oracle Red Bull Racing, based in Milton Keynes, UK, entered Formula 1 in 2005. The team quickly rose to prominence, winning four consecutive Constructors’ Championships from 2010 to 2013. In the hybrid era, their partnership with Honda and driver Max Verstappen brought them back to championship-winning form.

Max Verstappen

Max Verstappen made his Formula 1 debut in 2015 at just 17 years old, becoming the youngest driver in the sport’s history. He joined Red Bull Racing in 2016, where he won his first race at the Spanish Grand Prix.

Team Achievements (Unordered List)

  • 4 Constructors’ Championships (2010–2013)
  • 6+ Drivers’ Championships (2010–2013, 2021–2025)
  • Over 100 Grand Prix victories
  • Innovation in aerodynamics and hybrid technology

Max Verstappen Championship Timeline (Ordered List)

  1. 2021 — First World Championship, dramatic finish in Abu Dhabi
  2. 2022 — Dominant season with record-breaking wins
  3. 2023 — Third consecutive title
  4. 2024 — Fourth championship, further cementing dominance
  5. 2025 — Defending champion and team leader

Formula 1 Glossary (Definition List)

DRS
Drag Reduction System — a flap on the rear wing that reduces drag, increasing speed on straights.
Pole Position
The first starting spot on the grid, awarded to the fastest qualifier.
Undercut
A pit stop strategy where a driver pits earlier to gain track position using fresher tires.
Constructor
The team responsible for designing and building the car, competing in the Constructors’ Championship.
